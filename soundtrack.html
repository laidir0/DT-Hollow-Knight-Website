The Operatic Scale of the Soundtrack

The score for this game truly captures the story in all of its aspect. Composed and peformed by Christopher Larkin, there are two albums for the game. One being apart of the initial release, the other containing soundtracks from the four DLC packs thoroughout the years. This entire track really proves how essential music is to video games and how well it helps immerse the player in Team Cherry's subtle yet rich storytelling.

"Hollow Knight: Original Soundtrack"

Release dates:

Digital: February 10, 2017 (Bandcamp), February 24, 2017(GOG, Humble Store)

Vinyl: April, 2018

"Hollow Knight: Gods & Nightmares"

Release dates:

Digital: August 9, 2018 (Bandcamp, GOG, Humble Store)

Vinyl: December, 2018

As you begin the game, the soundtrack blesses your ears with a soft, somber piano, echoing how vast and empty this once thriving kingdom once was. As you progress the story, and uncover the dark secrets, the score gets more intense, as the orchestral soundtracks kick in at full force.

Some of my personal favourites from these soundtracks include:

"Broken Vessel": This marks the tale beginning to unravel. It offers a perspective the player might be beginning to realise; that this fallen kin has turned to the Infection and what that may spell for the entirety of Hallownest.

"Sealed Vessel": From the somber ambience that eerily follows the player as they delve deeper into the Black Egg, to the orchestral crescendo that accompanies the boss fight against the Hollow Knight: truly one of the best tracks this game offers. Encompasses the game as a whole for me.

"The Grimm Troupe": This is featured in the Nightmares DLC; and it really tells the player what they're in for when fighting Grimm for the first time in what I can only call a dance between mindsets. This track captures the excitment a festival brings.

Each score provides its own comfort, makes the world feel alive, even in its most emptiest of moments. But when that is removed, and all you are left with is the eeriness of the confined depths, you're left shaken, uncomftorable almost. I feel this is completely delibriate and quite honestly genius. The soundtrack adds such secruity that when you are without it you feel something is truly wrong.
